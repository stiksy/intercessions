# The Gospel in Genesis

**Date:** 23rd of November, 2025

**Bible Reading:** Hebrews 11:1-2 & 8-21

---

Let us pray.

When I say "Lord, in Your mercy," please respond with "hear our prayer."

Faithful God...
You called Abraham to step out in faith,
not knowing where he was going,
but trusting in Your promises.

You called a people to be Yours-
to be a blessing to all nations,
to carry Your promise through generations,
to point forward to Christ.

Even in Genesis... the gospel was taking shape.

Help us to walk by faith, not by sight...
to trust Your promises even when we can't see the outcome...
and to remember that we too are part of Your unfolding story.

Lord, in Your mercy...
hear our prayer.

Today we give thanks for Raymond Cole,
celebrating his milestone birthday with us.
We thank You for his life, his faith, and his journey with You.
As we share in this thanksgiving offering,
we praise You for Your goodness to Ray and to all of us.

May this celebration remind us that every good gift comes from You,
and that our lives are cause for thanksgiving.

Lord, in Your mercy...
hear our prayer.

We lift before You all who are suffering-
in body, mind, or spirit.
For those going through treatment...
for those waiting on diagnoses...
for those managing pain or facing uncertainty...

We pray especially for our former minister, Richard Turnbull,
and for all who, like him, trust in You even in the face of fear.

Lord, be near to them.
Grant them strength, comfort, and Your peace that surpasses understanding.
Surround them with Your presence,
and uphold all those who care for them.

Lord, in Your mercy...
hear our prayer.

We pray for this church and the work of our Council.
Thank You for this building-
a place that serves not just us, but our whole community.

We lift up the decisions being made-
about our finances, our mission, and the care of this place.
Grant wisdom to those who lead,
and guide us all in faithful stewardship.

Lord, You know our needs before we ask.
We trust You to provide.

We give thanks for the offerings we can share-
supporting mission around the world
and Christian ministries here in our town.
May these gifts bear fruit for Your kingdom.

Lord, in Your mercy...
hear our prayer.

As we look ahead to Christmas and the end of the year,
we pray for all the events being planned
and all the preparations already underway.
For everyone giving their time, energy, and creativity-
grant them joy in serving,
strength when they're tired,
and a deep sense of Your presence in it all.

May these celebrations draw people closer to You,
and may they reflect the good news of Jesus-
the promise fulfilled,
the Word made flesh.

Lord, in Your mercy...
hear our prayer.

We pray for those who are struggling-
those facing grief or loneliness as the year draws to a close.
For those carrying financial burdens...
for those who find this season difficult...
Be close to them.

And help us, as Your people,
to notice... to care... and to act.

Lord, in Your mercy...
hear our prayer.

And finally, faithful God,
help us to live as people of faith-
not because we have all the answers,
but because we know the one who does.

Like Abraham, may we step out when You call,
trusting that Your promises are sure...
and that You are leading us home.

We bring these prayers in the name of Jesus,
as we say together the prayer He taught us.

Feel free to do so in whatever language or version you're most comfortable with.

Our Father in heaven,
hallowed be Your name,
Your kingdom come,
Your will be done,
on earth as in heaven.
Give us today our daily bread.
Forgive us our sins
as we forgive those who sin against us.
Lead us not into temptation
but deliver us from evil.
For the kingdom, the power,
and the glory are Yours
now and forever.
Amen.
