# What Technology would God use?

**Date:** 11th of January, 2026

**Bible Reading:** Job 28

---

Let us pray.

God of all wisdom and grace...
In Job we read of humanity searching for treasure-
mining the earth, moving mountains, cutting channels through rock,
seeking precious things hidden in the depths.

We have not stopped searching.
We design, we innovate, we create tools to reshape our world.
And yet... true wisdom is not found in what we make.

Wisdom begins with reverence for You.
Understanding comes from knowing Your ways.

Help us to hold this tension well-
to use the gifts and tools You've given us with faithfulness,
but never to place our trust in them...
to create and to build,
but always to remember that You alone are the source of all good things.

We pray for Your global Church,
scattered across every nation,
using every language and every means to proclaim Your name.

We lift up especially our brothers and sisters in China
and in all places where following You comes at great cost.

Strengthen them.
Sustain them.
May they know that they are not alone,
that Your Spirit moves even where doors are closed.

We pray for all those affected by flu and winter illnesses, and for those
awaiting, undergoing, or recovering from tests and treatment.

Grant healing where You will,
comfort in the waiting,
and strength for each day.

Be also near to those supporting loved ones affected by health issues.
Give them patience, grace, and moments of rest.

We give thanks for this church-
for the life we share together, for the worship and fellowship,
for the ways You provide through the generosity of Your people.

We are grateful for the gifts that have sustained us,
for the mission and ministry we've been able to support,
for the care of this building and all who use it.

As we look ahead, we ask for continued provision.
Stir our hearts to give generously,
not out of anxiety, but out of joyful participation in what You are doing.

Guide our Finance Group in their stewardship -
may we trust not in our reserves,
but in You-the God who provides.

Bless the Christingle service next week-
may children and families encounter You there.

And we pray for the Congregational Meeting at the end of this month,
and for wisdom in all the decisions being made for the life of this church.

We also lift up the work of the Camrose Centre and the Night Light Winter Shelter-
for those who serve and for those in need.
Help us to reflect Your love in practical ways.

We pray for all those who feel the weight of this season-
for those facing financial strain,
for those who are lonely or grieving,
for those who are uncertain about the future.

Help us, as Your people, to notice and to care.

And finally, God of all wisdom,
teach us to seek You above all else.

In a world full of tools and noise and endless information,
give us discernment to know what truly matters.

May we use what we have been given-
our time, our resources, our creativity-
not for our own glory, but for Yours.

Keep our hearts rooted in reverence for You-
the beginning of all true wisdom.

We bring these prayers in the name of Jesus,
as we say together the prayer He taught us.

Our Father in heaven,  
hallowed be Your name,  
Your kingdom come,  
Your will be done,  
on earth as in heaven.  
Give us today our daily bread.  
Forgive us our sins  
as we forgive those who sin against us.  
Lead us not into temptation  
but deliver us from evil.  
For the kingdom, the power,  
and the glory are Yours  
now and forever.  
Amen.
