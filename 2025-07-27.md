# Meals with Jesus: At Martha and Mary's

**Date:** 27th of July, 2025

**Bible Reading:** Luke 10:38-42

---

Heavenly Father,

As we come to You in prayer, we're reminded of that day when Jesus was welcomed into the home of Martha and Mary. Help us, like Mary, to sit at Your feet with still hearts — to listen, to learn, and to draw close to You. And shape our service, like Martha's, not with anxiety, but with joy and purpose.

We thank You for last week's International Sunday — a beautiful time of worship and fellowship. Thank You for the diversity of voices and cultures that make up this church. We celebrate the unity we find in Christ, who welcomes us all to His table.

Thank You also for the improvements being made to our church building. For every person contributing time, skill, or resources — we are grateful. May this space always reflect hospitality, safety, and a deep sense of welcome, just like the home in Bethany where Jesus was received. And for the changes and improvements yet to come, help our leaders in making clear decisions, managing resources wisely, and carrying out each step with care and purpose.

As we look to the summer ahead, we lift up all the events being planned — especially the Holiday Bible Club beginning this week. We ask for joy, discovery, and encounters with You for every child attending. And for the organisers and volunteers: grant them strength, creativity, and moments of rest amidst the busyness.

We also pray for the events coming later this year. Guide every decision and every preparation. May they reflect not only good planning, but also deep dependence on You.

Father, we pray for deeper fellowship — especially for those who are new among us. May they be seen, welcomed, and drawn into the life of this church. May they find opportunities to meet in small groups, to share stories and life, and to pray together.

As we lift our eyes beyond these walls, we remember our brothers and sisters around the world — especially those living in the midst of war, persecution, or hardship. We pray for peace, justice and comfort. Strengthen Your global Church to be a witness of hope, even in dark places.

We bring before You all those who are struggling with illness — in body or in mind. Grant them healing, comfort, and renew their strength.

Lord, as a church, help us not to be distracted by many things — but to choose what is better: to seek You first. Teach us to live from that place of communion with You, where our service flows from stillness, and our busyness bows to Your voice.

Now, united in heart and purpose, we pray as Jesus taught us, saying:

Our Father in heaven,
hallowed be your name,
your kingdom come,
your will be done,
on earth as in heaven.
Give us today our daily bread.
Forgive us our sins
as we forgive those who sin against us.
Lead us not into temptation
but deliver us from evil.
For the kingdom, the power,
and the glory are yours
now and forever.
Amen.
