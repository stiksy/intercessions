# A Psalm Prayer for Councillors

**Date:** 3rd of May, 2026

**Bible Reading:** Psalm 138

---

Let us pray.

God of steadfast love...  
With our whole hearts we come before You today,  
as generations before us have done-  
bowing toward Your holy place,  
giving thanks for Your faithfulness.

Psalm 138 reminds us that when we call, You answer.  
That though You are exalted above all,  
You see the lowly,  
and You know the proud from afar.

We pray for all who serve in public life-  
for those who stand in local elections this week,  
and for all who currently serve as local councillors.

Whatever their party or persuasion,  
they are seeking to serve their neighbours.  
Grant them courage to pursue what is right,  
wisdom to know what is true,  
and humility to hear those they represent.  
May they govern with Your justice and Your compassion,  
and may they seek the flourishing of every person in their care-  
especially those who are overlooked,  
those on the margins,  
those whose voices are rarely heard.

For the Psalmist says: though the Lord is exalted,  
He looks kindly on the lowly.

We give thanks today for our new Church Council-  
for those who have answered the call to serve.  
We are especially grateful for the provision of a new Treasurer-  
a gift we had been praying for.  
Thank You, Lord, for Your faithfulness.

Grant wisdom and grace to each one who serves:  
in administration, in representation, in safeguarding.  
May they lead with integrity and care for this community with love.  
And we give thanks for those who have stepped down-  
for their years of faithful service.  
May they rest knowing their work was not in vain.

We pray for the season of prayer we are entering together.  
Through Pray in May, unite us with churches across Basingstoke  
in turning toward You for our town.  
May prayer be more than a discipline this month-  
may it be a delight.

Lord, we bring before You all children in fragile health-  
especially those born too soon,  
cared for in hospital,  
and the parents who wait and watch and hope.  
You are the God who preserves life.  
You see each tiny one and You love them.  
We ask for Your healing presence,  
for strength and peace for all who care for them,  
and for the medical staff who tend to them with skill.  
May all who face this long and uncertain road  
know that they do not walk alone.

We lift before You all in our church family who are suffering-  
those awaiting, undergoing, or recovering from tests and treatment,  
those living with long-term illness or pain.

We pray for those supporting loved ones afflicted by illness-  
carrying that weight day after day.  
Grant them patience, grace, and moments of genuine rest.

We remember all who grieve,  
those who have been recently bereaved.  
Comfort them with Your presence,  
and surround them with the love of this community.

We pray for Jonathan and Martha, currently on sabbatical-  
may it be a season of genuine rest and renewal,  
and may they return refreshed and ready.

And so, God of steadfast love...  
as the Psalm ends with this confidence-  
that Your love endures forever,  
that You will not abandon the works of Your hands-

We trust You with all we have named today.  
With the elections and the new council,  
with vulnerable little ones and their families,  
with the grieving and the sick,  
with this church and its future.

Your purpose for us stands.  
Your love endures.

We bring these prayers in the name of Jesus,  
as we say together the prayer He taught us.

Feel free to do so in whatever language or version you're most comfortable with.

Our Father in heaven,  
hallowed be Your name,  
Your kingdom come,  
Your will be done,  
on earth as in heaven.  
Give us today our daily bread.  
Forgive us our sins  
as we forgive those who sin against us.  
Lead us not into temptation  
but deliver us from evil.  
For the kingdom, the power,  
and the glory are Yours  
now and forever.  
Amen.
