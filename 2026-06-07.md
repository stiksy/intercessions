# Jesus Speaks Truth to Power

**Date:** 7 June 2026

**Bible Reading:** Matthew 23:1-36

---

Let us pray.
*When I say "Lord, in your mercy," please respond with "hear our prayer."*

Lord God, You are the God who sees beneath the surface...
who looks past outward show to the state of our hearts...
In Jesus, You spoke truth with courage and love -
not to condemn, but to call us back
from performance to genuine faith,
from the letter of the law to its living spirit...

We come to You as a community that is trying -
sometimes well, sometimes badly -
to walk what we talk...
to let our yes mean yes,
and our service be genuine...

---

We pray for Your Church throughout the world...
Guard her from the temptation to do right things for the wrong reasons -
to seek recognition rather than to serve,
to make faith a performance rather than a practice...

Help us in this congregation to carry one another's burdens
rather than adding to them...
to lead with humility rather than position...
to pursue justice and mercy and faithfulness -
the weightier matters that Jesus named...

Lord, in your mercy...
*hear our prayer.*

---

We give You thanks for Jonathan and Martha,
and we lift them to You as they prepare to return from sabbatical next week.
May they come back refreshed and renewed,
ready again for the work You have called them to...

And we thank You for all who have faithfully served in their absence -
stepping up quietly, not for applause but out of love for You
and for this community...
may they know how deeply their service has been felt...

Lord, in your mercy...
*hear our prayer.*

---

We bring to You all who are walking through hard and heavy seasons...
those living with illness, facing tests and treatment,
or carrying the weight of long recovery...
those whose pain is hidden - emotional, mental, spiritual...

Lord, in your mercy...
*hear our prayer.*

---

We give thanks for all among us marking a birthday or anniversary in these days -
another year received as gift,
a milestone that quietly speaks of Your faithfulness...

Bless them with a fresh awareness of Your goodness,
and may the year ahead be full of Your presence and grace...

Lord, in your mercy...
*hear our prayer.*

---

Lord, Jesus called us to be servants, not to be seen...
to give from the heart, not for applause...

As we go from this place,
may our faith not end when the service does -
may it continue in our homes, our streets, our workplaces,
in acts of genuine love that ask nothing in return...

And now, as Your people across all times and places have done,
let us bring all of this before You in the prayer that Jesus taught us.
Feel free to do so in whatever language or version you're most comfortable with.

*Our Father in heaven,
hallowed be your name,
your kingdom come,
your will be done,
on earth as in heaven.
Give us today our daily bread.
Forgive us our sins
as we forgive those who sin against us.
Lead us not into temptation
but deliver us from evil.
For the kingdom, the power,
and the glory are yours
now and for ever. Amen.*
