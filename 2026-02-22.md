# The Faithful Investor: Treasure

**Date:** 22nd of February, 2026

**Bible Reading:** Matthew 6:19-34

---

Let us pray.

Generous God...  
You tell us not to store up treasures on earth,  
where moth and rust destroy,  
and where thieves break in and steal.

You invite us instead to store up treasures in heaven-  
where nothing can corrupt and nothing can be taken away.

For where our treasure is... there our heart will be also.

In these past weeks You have been teaching us about stewardship-  
about our hearts, our love, our time, and our talents.  
Now we bring before You the matter of our treasure.

Help us, Lord, to hold what we have with open hands...  
to see our resources not as ours alone,  
but as gifts entrusted to us for Your purposes.

We thank You for those who give so generously of their time  
to the life of this church.  
For the leaders of our children's groups-  
the newly commissioned Preschool Team  
and the Sparklers team returning after half term-  
thank You for their love, their patience,  
and their faithfulness in nurturing the youngest among us.

We thank You too for those who work behind the scenes-  
quietly, consistently, without seeking recognition.  
Those who keep things running so that others can be welcomed...  
for every act of service done in Your name that no one sees.

You see it, Lord. And we give thanks.

We are grateful for all who have recently responded to the call to serve-  
stepping into new roles with willing hearts.  
Keep providing, Lord.  
Stir in others a desire to offer what they can-  
in prayer, in administration, in serving-  
so that the work of this church may continue to flourish.

We pray for those gathering today for the Newcomers Welcome session.  
May it be a place of warmth and genuine connection.  
Help those who are new among us to feel truly at home-  
not as visitors, but as family.

May they catch a glimpse of Your heart for this community,  
and may they know that they belong here...  
that they are wanted, valued, and loved.

As we enter this season of Lent,  
we pray for each of us as we seek to draw closer to You.  
Whether through fasting, prayer, or acts of service-  
may this be a season of honest reflection and renewed devotion.

Help us to hear Your voice more clearly,  
to let go of what distracts us,  
and to fix our eyes on what truly matters.

We lift before You all who are suffering-  
in body, mind, or spirit.  
For those going through treatment or awaiting results...  
for those managing pain or uncertainty...  
for those supporting loved ones affected by dementia  
or other long-term conditions...

Be near to them, Lord.  
Grant them strength for today  
and hope for tomorrow.  
Surround them with Your peace,  
and uphold those who care for them.

We pray for the leadership of this church  
as preparations are made for the months ahead.  
Grant wisdom and unity to those who lead,  
and guide us all as we seek to follow Your calling-  
each finding the right place to serve.

And so, faithful God...  
the birds of the air do not sow or reap,  
yet You feed them.  
The flowers of the field do not labour or spin,  
yet You clothe them in splendour.

How much more will You care for us.

Teach us not to worry about tomorrow,  
but to seek first Your kingdom and Your righteousness,  
trusting that all we need will be given.

May we be faithful investors-  
placing our treasure where it matters most...  
in Your kingdom,  
in Your people,  
and in Your purposes.

We bring these prayers in the name of Jesus,  
as we say together the prayer He taught us.

Feel free to do so in whatever language or version you're most comfortable with.

Our Father in heaven,  
hallowed be Your name,  
Your kingdom come,  
Your will be done,  
on earth as in heaven.  
Give us today our daily bread.  
Forgive us our sins  
as we forgive those who sin against us.  
Lead us not into temptation  
but deliver us from evil.  
For the kingdom, the power,  
and the glory are Yours  
now and forever.  
Amen.
