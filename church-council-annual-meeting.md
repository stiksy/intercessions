# Church Council and Annual Meeting

**Date:**

**Bible Reading:**

---

God of all wisdom and grace...
You have made us one body in Christ—
each with a part to play,
each with gifts to share,
each called to love... and to serve.

Help us to follow the example of Jesus—
who led by serving,
who taught by listening,
who welcomed the overlooked...
and challenged the powerful.

Lord, we pray for your Church—here and across the world.
In all our diversity and difference...
help us to stay united in your love and your truth.

Where there is tension... bring peace.
Where there is weariness... bring strength.
Where there is apathy... rekindle purpose.

Make us bold in faith...
and gentle in spirit.

Today we especially thank you
for those who have served on our church council.

For their time...
their commitment...
and their love for this church family—
we are truly grateful.

As some step down from their roles,
may they find rest, encouragement,
and joy in what they've helped build.

And for those preparing to join or continue on the council—
give them clarity of mind,
unity with others,
and hearts open to your Spirit.

Guide them as they help shape the life of our church.

And as we meet after this service,
may our Annual Meeting be filled with respect... listening...
and shared hope.

Lord, we pray for the young people in our community—
for those trying to figure out who they are...
for those feeling pressure to perform or fit in...
and for those who feel invisible.

May they know your love,
may they find support,
and may this church always be a place
where they feel safe... and seen.

We also remember those that are going through their GCSEs - we pray for them to remain focused but also calm in this time of high pressure.

We bring before you those who are struggling—
those who are grieving...
those living with illness...
those battling anxiety, depression, or despair.
You know each story. Each burden. Each name……….
Be near to them.
And help us, as your people,
to show up with kindness... and care.

And finally, Lord, we pray for ourselves.
Give us courage to serve where we're needed,
humility to let others lead when it's their turn,
and joy in knowing we're part of something bigger than ourselves.

Keep us rooted in love...
anchored in hope...
and open to where your Spirit is leading next.

We bring these prayers to you in the name of Jesus, as we say together the prayer that He taught us.

Feel free to do it in whatever form or language you're most comfortable with.

Our Father in heaven,
hallowed be your name,
your kingdom come,
your will be done,
on earth as in heaven.
Give us today our daily bread.
Forgive us our sins
as we forgive those who sin against us.
Lead us not into temptation
but deliver us from evil.
For the kingdom, the power,
and the glory are yours
now and forever.
Amen.
