# Promised Blessings

**Date:** 21st of September, 2025

**Bible Reading:** Genesis 12:1-5; 15:1-6; 17:1-8

---

In our readings and in Nigel's message today we heard how God's promised blessings shaped Abraham's life and generations to come; let us now bring our prayers, trusting in those same promises for our world, our church, and ourselves.

**Loving God**, You promised blessings to Abraham and his descendants,
and through Christ You bless the whole world.
We bring our prayers, trusting in Your faithfulness.

**God of promise**, we pray for a world that longs for blessing.
Where nations are at war, bring peace.
Where leaders cling to power, stir up justice.
Move us to be careful stewards of Your creation.
Bless all peoples with hope for a future of fairness and peace.

**God of comfort**, we remember all who feel anxious, forgotten, or without purpose.
For those burdened by illness or grief,
for refugees seeking safety,
for communities facing hardship.
We remember especially our former Christ Church minister, Richard, and our brother in faith, Mike. May Your promised blessing bring healing and comfort to all who need it, and also peace of mind for those around them, knowing that You are always on their side.

**God of covenant**, bless Your church across the world and in this community.
Make us a people of faith and generosity,
ready to trust Your call and to share Your blessing with others.
We put into Your hands the church council meetings in the next few days. Guide our leaders and strengthen all who serve in the work of Your Church.

**God of grace**, as You called Abraham to set out in faith,
so call us to trust You in our daily lives.
Bless us with courage where we hesitate,
with joy where we are weary,
and with hope that shines out to those around us.

**Faithful God**, You are the giver of every good promise.
Hold us in Your love and bless us through Jesus Christ our Lord.

Gathering up all our prayers, and trusting that God's blessings are for every generation and every person, we join together in the prayer Jesus taught us. And feel free to do so in whatever language or version you're most comfortable with.

Our Father in heaven,
hallowed be Your name,
Your kingdom come,
Your will be done,
on earth as in heaven.
Give us today our daily bread.
Forgive us our sins
as we forgive those who sin against us.
Lead us not into temptation
but deliver us from evil.
For the kingdom, the power,
and the glory are Yours
now and forever.
Amen.
